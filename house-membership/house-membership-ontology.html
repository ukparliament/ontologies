HouseSeatc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://parliament.uk/ontologies/house-membership/HouseSeat

A seat in a House. A place reserved for a Member in a House. This does not equate to a constituency. A seat might be associated with a constituency (in the House of Commons) but might be allocated to a bishop or inherited by an hereditary peer.

A house seat type. This includes first past the post elected in the Commons. In the Lords it includes hereditary peers, life peers, excepted hereditary peers and Lords spiritual (made up of named diocesan and long serving English diocesan bishops).

swearingInOrderdp back to ToC or Data Property ToC

IRI: https://parliament.uk/ontologies/house-membership/swearingInOrder

The order in which Members take the oath (religious) or affirmation (secular) at the beginning of a new Parliament or following a by-election. Swearing in order can be used to determine who is the longest serving member of a House. The longest serving Member of a House is known as the Father or Mother of the House.
has domain
SeatIncumbencyc
has range
integer
