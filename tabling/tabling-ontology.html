TabledThingc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/tabling/TabledThing

Any item of business which has been tabled e.g. a motion, parliamentary question, amendment or nomination. Note that not all motions are tabled e.g. manuscript amendments are amendments which are raised on the day the business occurs. In other words, no notice has been given. For this reason they do not appear on the day's business papers and are rarely selected for debate. Manuscript amendments do not appear on the marshalled list.
is in domain of
replacesop
is in range of
tablesop

Tablingc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/tabling/Tabling

The act of tabling a business item into either the House of Commons or House of Lords. Tabling is the act of formally putting forward a question, a motion, an amendment or a nomination. Members do this by submitting it to procedural clerks. The item will then appear in the next day's business papers as a written notice. Each House has a Table Office where parliamentary questions, motions for debate and, in the Commons, early day motions are tabled. Amendments to bills are always tabled in the Public Bill Offices of each House.
has super-classes
TablingOrWithdrawalc
is in domain of
givesNoticeop, isInterestDeclareddp, tabledOndp, tablesop
is in range of
requestsop

isInterestDeclareddp back to ToC or Data Property ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/tabling/isInterestDeclared

Relates an act of tabling to whether or not the Member doing the tabling declared an interest at the time it was tabled. Note that this is a boolean and does not describe or link to any definition of the nature of the interest. The interest declared against a tabling may or may not relate to an interest registered on the Register of Members' Financial Interests.
has domain
Tablingc
has range
boolean
