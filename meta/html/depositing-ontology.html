Depositing ontology

A model describing the commitment to make and the making of a deposit in the Library of either House.

Classes · Object properties · Data properties · Subclasses · Namespaces · Depiction

Classes

Class Availability type

The type of the availability of a deposited paper, being: available to the public, available to the public in a redacted form, or available only to Members.

Class Commitment

A commitment to make a deposit in the Library of a House.

Class Committing thing

The means by which a commitment to deposit is made, for example: a contribution to a parliamentary debate, a letter to a Library or a written statement.

Class Credited contribution

An oral contribution made by a known Member during a parliamentary proceeding.

Class Deposited artefact

An artefact which has been deposited in the Library of a House, for example: a breathalyser.

Class Incumbency

The holding of a position by a person, including the time period for which this was the case. For example, Philip Hammond holding the position of Foreign Secretary between two dates.

Class Made available thing

A thing made available to a House or to a House by means of its Library.

Class Made statutory instrument work

Made statutory instruments as signed into legislation. Made statutory instruments may result from a draft laid before Parliament or may be laid as made or may not be laid if the enabling legislation does not set out a duty to lay. Made statutory instruments laid before Parliament may be subject to a negative, affirmative or super affirmative procedure, or none. A made negative, having not been objected to within the scrutiny period, remains law. A made affirmative or made super affirmative, having been approved by Parliament within the approval period, remains law.

Class Making available

The act of making a thing available to a House or to a House by means of its Library.

Class Paper expression

A paper made available to a House, being an expression of a paper work.

Class Paper series item

A paper expression or artefact, as it exists as part of a paper series. A paper expression or artefact forming part of a series has an identifying number within that series.

Class Parliamentary period

The time period of a single Parliament between the date of its first assembly after a general election and its dissolution. A Parliament period contains contiguous session and prorogation periods. During a parliament period Parliament is either in session or prorogued. The start date of a Parliament may be changed by subsequent proclamations. A Parliament ends at the next dissolution. While the Fixed-term Parliaments Act 2011 was in force, the date of dissolution was determined by that Act or was named in a proclamation following a vote in Parliament for an early general election. The next Parliament began on the date named in the proclamation issued following a dissolution. Before the Fixed-term Parliaments Act 2011 came into force and after that Act was repealed by the Dissolution and Calling of Parliament Act 2022, the date of dissolution is fixed by proclamation. The dissolution proclamation also names the date for Parliament to reassemble. Parliament periods are the same across both Houses. Parliaments are numbered ordinally from the first Parliament of the United Kingdom, sitting in 1801.

Object property Has availability type

Relates a deposited paper to the availability type of that deposited paper. A deposited paper has one availability type.

Object property Leads to

Relates a credited contribution in which the intention to make a commitment is recorded to the thing in which that commitment is made. Credited contributions may include the phrase, 'I will write'. A 'will write' is a contribution intended to lead to a letter of commitment to the Library, that letter being the committing thing. A letter of commitment may result from one credited contribution, or none.

CreditedContribution — Leads to — CommittingThing

Object property Made by

Relates a commitment to the incumbency of a person in a position making that commitment. A commitment is made by one incumbency.

Commitment — Made by — Incumbency

Object property Made during

Relates a commitment to the parliamentary period during which the commitment is made. A commitment is made during one parliamentary period.

Commitment — Made during — ParliamentaryPeriod

Object property Results from commitment

Relates a deposit to the commitment to make that deposit. A commitment is made to provide one or more deposits. A deposit results from one commitment, or none. Where a deposit does not result from a commitment, it results from a duty set out in legislation.

Depositing — Results from commitment — Commitment

Object property Results from duty set out in

Relates a deposit to the made statutory instrument setting out the duty under which that deposit is made. Transport and Works Act order applications are deposited under a duty set out in Rule 13 of The Transport and Works (Applications and Objections Procedure) (England and Wales) Rules 2006. Where a deposit does not result from a duty set out in legislation, it results from a commitment to make a deposit.

Depositing — Results from duty set out in — MadeStatutoryInstrumentWork

Object property Results in deposited artefact

Relates an act of depositing to the artefact or artefacts which have been deposited. A deposited artefact is the result of one deposit. An act of depositing results in one or more deposited artefacts, or none. Where an act of depositing does not result in a deposited artefact, it results in a deposited paper or papers.

Depositing — Results in deposited artefact — DepositedArtefact

Object property Results in deposited paper

Relates an act of depositing to the paper or papers which have been deposited. A deposited paper is the result of one deposit. An act of depositing results in one or more deposited papers, or none. Where an act of depositing does not result in a deposited paper, it results in a deposited artefact or artefacts.

Depositing — Results in deposited paper — DepositedPaper
