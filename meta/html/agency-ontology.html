Agency ontology

A model describing people and groups of people having agency. The model also describes membership of and positions in groups.

Classes · Object properties · Data properties · Subclasses · Namespaces · Depiction

Classes

Class Agent

Agents being people and groups of people.

Class Group

A group of people. A group may have no members at a point in time.

Superclasses · Agent

Class Group inclusion

The inclusion of a person or group in another group over a period of time, for example: a person being a member of a committee or a subcommittee being part of a committee.

Class Incumbency

The holding of a position by a person over a period of time. For example, Philip Hammond holding the position of Foreign Secretary between two dates.

Class Person

A person.

Superclasses · Agent

Class Position

A named post within a group. A position may be held by one or more people over time or at the same time, or have no holder at a particular time: for example, the post of Minister of Food, having existed in the past and having had eight successive incumbents; or a Member of the House of Commons and a Member of the House of Lords both serving as Co-Chairs of the Joint Committee on the Palace of Westminster.

Class Position in group

The association between a position and the group that position is part of for a period of time. For example: the position of 'Minister for Women and Equalities' being part of the Department for International Trade from October 2022 to July 2024, then being part of the Department for Education from July 2024.

Object properties

Object property In group

Relates a 'position in a group' to the group. A 'position in a group' is in one group. A group may have one or more 'positions in groups' over time, or none.

PositionInGroup — In group — Group

Object property In position

Relates an incumbency to a position. An incumbency is to a single position. A position may have one or more incumbencies, or none over time. A position may have more than one incumbency at a given time, for example: a committee chair position having two incumbents at the same time in the case of Co-Chairs.

Incumbency — In position — Position

Object property Inclusion in group

Relates a group inclusion to a group. A group inclusion is in a single group. A group may have one or more group inclusions, or none.

GroupInclusion — Inclusion in group — Group

Object property Inclusion of agent

Relates a group inclusion to an agent. A group inclusion is of a single agent. An agent may have one or more group inclusions, or none.

GroupInclusion — Inclusion of agent — Agent

Object property Of person

Relates an incumbency to the person occupying that incumbency. A person may have one or more incumbencies, or none. An incumbency is of one person.

Object property Of position
