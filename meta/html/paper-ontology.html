Paper ontology

A model describing papers, their types and their presence in bundles and in series.

Classes · Object properties · Data properties · Namespaces · Depiction

Classes

Class Author

The author of a paper, being either a person or a corporate body.

Class Paper bundle

A group of related paper expressions, called a bundle, for example: a bundle containing a statutory instrument paper and a related explanatory memorandum.

Class Paper expression

A paper made available to a House, being an expression of a paper work.

Class Paper series

A series of papers, for example: the Treaty Series.

Class Paper series membership

The membership of a paper expression in a paper series. A paper expression being a member of a series has an identifying number within that series.

Class Paper type

The type of a paper, for example: bill, statutory instrument, petition or statement of annual accounts.

Class Paper work

A paper as a single intellectual work, as defined in the Functional Requirements for Bibliographic Records specification.

Class Session

A period during which Parliament is able to conduct business on days of its choosing. A session is within a Parliament period. The first session of a Parliament begins on the same day as the Parliament period and ends on a day named in a subsequent prorogation proclamation. Subsequent sessions within the Parliament period begin and end on dates named in subsequent prorogation proclamations. The final session of a Parliament ends either with prorogation followed by dissolution or with dissolution. Session periods are the same for both Houses.

Object property Belongs to

Object property Expression of

Relates a paper expression to the paper work that paper is an expression of. A paper expression is an expression of one paper work. A paper work is expressed as one or more papers.

PaperExpression — Expression of — PaperWork

Object property Forms part of

Relates a paper expression to the paper bundle it forms part of. A paper expression may belong to one or more paper bundles, or none. For example: a single explanatory memorandum may form part of more than one bundle.

PaperExpression — Forms part of — PaperBundle

Object property Membership in series

Relates the membership of a paper expression in a series to the series that membership forms part of. A paper series membership forms part of one paper series.

PaperSeriesMembership — Membership in series — PaperSeries

Object property Membership of paper expression

Relates a paper series membership to the paper expression with that membership. A paper series membership refers to one paper expression. A paper expression may be a member of one or more series, or none.

PaperSeriesMembership — Membership of paper expression — PaperExpression

Object property Paper series follows

Relates a paper series to its preceding series. A paper series may follow one series, or none. A paper series may be followed by one series, or none, for example: the series of statutory instruments made in 2022 follows the series of statutory instruments made in 2021.

PaperSeries — Paper series follows — PaperSeries

Object property Paper series parent

Relates a paper series to its parent paper series. A paper series may have one parent paper series, or none. A paper series may have one or more child paper series, or none, for example: the Treaty Series of in force treaties is a child series of the Command Papers series.

PaperSeries — Paper series parent — PaperSeries

Object property Paper type parent

Relates one paper type to another in a parent-child relationship. A paper type may have one or more parent paper types, or none. A paper type may have one or more child paper types, or none.
