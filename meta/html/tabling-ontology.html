Tabling ontology

A model describing the act of tabling by a Member.

Classes · Object properties · Data properties · Subclasses · Namespaces · Depiction

Classes

Class Event

An event taking place inside or outside Parliament. For example: a second reading debate, the tabling of an early day motion, a laying of a paper or the making of a statutory instrument by a government minister.

Class House

A parliamentary House, for example: the House of Lords.

Class Incumbency

The holding of a position by a person, including the time period for which this was the case. For example, Philip Hammond holding the position of Foreign Secretary between two dates.

Class Session

A period during which Parliament is able to conduct business on days of its choosing. A session is within a Parliament period. The first session of a Parliament begins on the same day as the Parliament period and ends on a day named in a subsequent prorogation proclamation. Subsequent sessions within the Parliament period begin and end on dates named in subsequent prorogation proclamations. The final session of a Parliament ends either with prorogation followed by dissolution or with dissolution. Session periods are the same for both Houses.

Class Tabled thing

Any item of business which has been tabled, for example: a tabled motion, a tabled parliamentary question, a tabled amendment or a tabled committee membership nomination.

Class Tabling

The act of tabling a business item - being a question, a motion, an amendment or a committee membership nomination - into either the House of Commons or House of Lords. Members submit business items to the procedural offices. If accepted, the business item is considered to be tabled and will then appear as a written notice in the business papers for the next day. Each House has a Table Office where parliamentary questions, motions for debate and, in the Commons, early day motions are tabled. Amendments to bills are always tabled in the Public Bill Offices of each House.
