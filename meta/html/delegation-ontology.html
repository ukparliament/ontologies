Delegation ontology

A model describing the enabling of secondary legislation.

Classes

Class Act of Parliament

An Act of Parliament creates, amends or repeals legislation. An Act is a Bill having been passed by Parliament and been given Royal Assent. Acts of Parliament which are in force comprise statute law.

Superclasses · StatutoryThing · EnablingThing

Class Draft statutory instrument work

An instrument laid in draft before Parliament subject to either a negative, affirmative or super affirmative procedure. An instrument under the draft negative procedure may be made into law, if not objected to within the scrutiny period. An instrument under the draft affirmative procedure may be made into law, having been approved by Parliament. An instrument under the draft super affirmative procedure may be made into law, having been approved by Parliament within the approval period.

Superclasses · StatutoryInstrumentSubjectToParliamentaryProcedureWork

Class Enabled thing

A thing resulting from a Minister exercising or intending to exercise a power conferred by an enabling thing.

Superclasses · PaperWork

Class Enabling thing

A thing that enables the exercise of a statutory power.

Class Made statutory instrument work

Statutory instruments as signed into law. Made statutory instruments may result from a draft laid before Parliament, or may be laid as made, or may not be laid if the enabling legislation does not set out a duty to lay. Made statutory instruments laid before Parliament are subject to a negative, affirmative or super affirmative procedure, or none. An instrument under the made negative procedure remains law, unless objected to within the scrutiny period. An instrument under the made affirmative procedure or made super affirmative procedure remains law, if approved by Parliament within the approval period.

Superclasses · StatutoryInstrumentSubjectToParliamentaryProcedureWork · StatutoryThing

Class Paper work

A paper as a single intellectual work, as defined in the Functional Requirements for Bibliographic Records specification.

Class Proposed draft remedial order work

A proposal for a draft remedial order. Under paragraph 3(1) of schedule 2 of the Human Rights Act 1998, the Minister has a duty to lay before Parliament 'a document which contains a draft of the proposed order'. This document must lay before Parliament for a period of 60 sitting days, beginning with the day on which it was laid.

Superclasses · EnabledThing

Class Proposed negative statutory instrument work

A regulation made under a power or powers set out in clause 7, 8 or 9 of the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018, or under schedule 5 of the Future Relationship Act 2020, which government ministers propose be laid under the negative procedure. Sifting committees of either House may recommend that any subsequent statutory instrument or instruments be laid under the affirmative procedure.

Superclasses · EnabledThing

Class Published draft under the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018 work

A draft published under the duties laid out in paragraph 14 of schedule 8 of the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018. A Minister has a duty to publish such a draft 28 joint sitting days before the laying of an instrument intending to amend or revoke subordinate legislation under section 2(2) of the European Communities Act 1972, including subordinate legislation implementing EU directives.

Superclasses · EnabledThing

Class Statutory instrument subject to parliamentary procedure work

Statutory instruments subject to parliamentary procedure according to duties set out in legislation. Statutory instruments may be enabled by legislation delegating powers without requiring consideration by Parliament.

Superclasses · EnabledThing

Class Statutory thing

A statutory thing, for example: an Act of Parliament or a made statutory instrument.

Object property Enabled by

Relates an enabled thing to the enabling thing under which it was made or under which it is intended to be made. An enabled thing may be enabled by one or more enabling things. An enabling thing may enable one or more enabled things, or none.

Object property Preceded by

Relates an enabled thing to the enabled thing which preceded it. An enabled thing may be preceded by one or more enabled things, or by none. An enabled thing may precede one or more enabled things, or none. For example: a statutory instrument being preceded by a proposed negative statutory instrument.

Relates an enabled thing to the enabled thing which it supersedes. An enabled thing may be superseded by one or more enabled things, or none. An enabled thing may supersede one or more enabled things, or none. For example: a withdrawn statutory instrument being superseded by another statutory instrument.
