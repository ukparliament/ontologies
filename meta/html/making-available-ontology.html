Making available ontology

A model describing the act of making material available to a House and any subsequent withdrawal.

Classes · Object properties · Data properties · Subclasses · Namespaces · Depiction

Classes

Class House

A parliamentary House, for example: the House of Lords.

Class Incumbency

The holding of a position by a person, including the time period for which this was the case. For example, Philip Hammond holding the position of Foreign Secretary between two dates.

Class Made available thing

A thing made available to a House or to a House by means of its Library.

Class Making available

The act of making a thing available to a House or to a House by means of its Library.

Class Making available body

A body empowered to make materials available to a House: being a Government department, a Government organisation, a non-governmental organisation with a duty to answer questions, an organisation with a duty to lay papers or a committee.

Class Session

A period during which Parliament is able to conduct business on days of its choosing. A session is within a Parliament period. The first session of a Parliament begins on the same day as the Parliament period and ends on a day named in a subsequent prorogation proclamation. Subsequent sessions within the Parliament period begin and end on dates named in subsequent prorogation proclamations. The final session of a Parliament ends either with prorogation followed by dissolution or with dissolution. Session periods are the same for both Houses.

Class Withdrawal

The act of withdrawing a thing previously made available to a House or to a House by means of its Library.

Class Withdrawn thing

A thing withdrawn from a House, having previously been made available to that House.

Object property During

Relates an act of making a thing available to the session during which that thing is made available. A thing made available during prorogation is not made available during a session. An act of making available is during one session, or none.

Object property Made available by

Relates an act of making a thing available to the incumbency of the person in a position who makes that thing available, for example: the depositing of a paper by Philip Hammond as Foreign Secretary. An act of making available is by one person incumbent in one position.

MakingAvailable — Made available by — Incumbency

Object property Makes available

Relates an act of making a thing available to the thing being made available. The act of making available makes one thing available. A thing is made available by one act of making available.

MakingAvailable — Makes available — MadeAvailableThing

Object property Recorded in

Relates an act of making a thing available to the session in which that thing is recorded as having been made available. A thing made available during a prorogation is recorded as having been made available in the subsequent session. An act of making available is recorded in one session.

MakingAvailable — Recorded in — Session

Object property Results in

Relates an act of withdrawal to the thing having been withdrawn. An act of withdrawal results in one withdrawn thing. A withdrawn thing is the result of one act of withdrawal.

Object property To House

Relates an act of making a thing available to the House in which that thing is made available. An act of making available is to one House. A thing may be made available to both Houses by means of two acts of making available.

MakingAvailable — To House — House

Object property Withdrawn by

Relates an act of withdrawal to the incumbency of a person in a position performing the withdrawal. An act of withdrawal is performed by one incumbency.
