Procedure step ontology

A model describing annotations added to business steps in the procedure model by the Indexing and Data Management Section of the House of Commons Library. Annotations are used to describe how a business step is actualised.

Classes · Object properties · Data properties · Namespaces · Depiction

Classes

Class Business step

A step of type business. A business step describes a set of potential business items, for example: the potential for papers to be laid before the House of Commons. Potential business items described by a business step may take place inside or outside Parliament.

Class Publication

A publication, for example: UK Parliament Hansard.

Object properties

Object property Actualised alongside

Relates a business step to another business step where the two steps may be actualised alongside each other as a single business item. A business step may be actualised alongside one or more business steps, or none.

BusinessStep — Actualised alongside — BusinessStep

Object property Source

Relates a business step to the publication which is the source of information for the business item actualising that business step. A business step is sourced from one publication, or none.

BusinessStep — Source — Publication

Data properties

Data property Date note

A note describing the nature of the date that should be added to a business item actualising a business step. For example: a business item actualising a business step describing committee consideration should use the date that the committee met, rather than the date on which the committee report was published.

BusinessStep — Date note — langString

Data property Link note

A note describing the nature of the link that should be added to a business item actualising a business step. For example: a business item actualising a business step describing the putting of a question in the House of Commons should link to the corresponding business item recorded in Votes and Proceedings.

BusinessStep — Link note — langString
