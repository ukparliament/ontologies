Treaty ontology

A model describing treaties made available to Parliament.

Classes

Class Departmental lead

The Government department which has the leadership of a treaty. The Government department which has the leadership of a treaty may change following a 'machinery of Government' announcement.

Class Ministerial department

A ministerial department is an executive body of government, headed by a government minister having responsibility for described areas of public administration.

Class Paper expression

A paper made available to a House, being an expression of a paper work.

Class Paper work

A paper as a single intellectual work, as defined in the Functional Requirements for Bibliographic Records specification.

Class Treaty expression

An expression of a treaty work as laid before Parliament. Different treaty expressions may be laid before Parliament over time. Treaty works requiring ratification are subject to the Constitutional Reform and Governance Act 2010. The 2010 Act sets out that a treaty must be laid before Parliament prior to ratification. After the treaty has been laid, it is subject to the negative procedure described in part within the 2010 Act. After a treaty has been ratified, a copy of the treaty is laid before Parliament. Treaty works which do not require ratification may have a treaty expression laid before Parliament.

Class Treaty work

A treaty is a written agreement between states - or between states and international organisations - which is binding under international law.

Data properties

Data property UK coming into force date

The date on which a treaty is recorded as coming into force in the UK. Parts of a treaty may come into force on different dates and under differing conditions. Where no single coming into force date is given, coming into force details are given in a coming into force note.

Data property UK coming into force note

A coming into force note sets out details applying to the UK of the commencement of provisions or effects forming part or parts of a treaty, or the treaty as a whole where it does not come into force on a single date.
