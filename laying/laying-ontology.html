LayingAuthorityTypec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://parliament.uk/ontologies/laying/LayingAuthorityType

The procedural authority by which a paper is laid before a House. Many documents are required by law to be laid before the House. These are called Act papers and are referred to as being laid 'by Act'. Other papers are made available to the House by the Government. These are called Command papers and are referred to as being laid 'by Command'. A paper cannot be laid by both Act and Command, although in some cases papers are laid by Act in the Commons and by Command in the Lords.

layingAuthorityTypeop

Sessionc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://parliament.uk/ontologies/laying/Session

The period during which Parliament is active. The first session of a parliament begins on the same day as the parliament. This date is named in the dissolution proclamation. It ends on the day named in the subsequent prorogation proclamation. Subsequent sessions begin and end on dates named in prorogation proclamations. The final session ends with a prorogation followed by a dissolution. Parliament may be prorogued a few days before dissolution. A session typically lasts for one year though isn't bound by that. Session periods are the same across both Houses.
is in range of
inSessionop

enabledByop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: https://parliament.uk/ontologies/laying/enabledBy

Relates a layable thing to the legislation that enables it. Note that this might be either primary or secondary legislation. Where it's secondary legislation, that legislation must in turn be enabled by an Act, possibly via other pieces of secondary legislation. The enabling Act will set out what changes are permissable by secondary legislation. The Joint Committee on Statutory Instruments (or the Select Committee on Statutory Instruments for papers laid in the House of Commons only) will examine secondary legislation laid before Parliament, advising on whether or not it fits within the bounds specified by the enabling Act.
has domain
LayableThingc
has range
Legislationc
