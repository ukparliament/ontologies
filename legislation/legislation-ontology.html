PrimaryLegislationc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/PrimaryLegislation

An item of primary legislation. Types of primary legislation include Acts of Parliament, Church of England Measures, and Orders in Council made under Royal Prerogative. Primary legislation sets out broad outlines and principles, and delegates specific authority to government to make more specific laws in the form of secondary legislation.

ProposedStatutoryInstrumentc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/ProposedStatutoryInstrument

All regulations proposed to be made under the powers in clauses 7, 8 or 9 of the European Union (Withdrawal) Bill, which Ministers consider need to be made under the negative procedure, will be laid before both Houses as proposed statutory instruments. Sifting committees in both Houses may recommend a change to the affirmative procedure. A proposed statutory instrument may lead to a statutory instrument being laid, or multiple, or none.
is in range of

SecondaryLegislationc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/SecondaryLegislation

An item of secondary legislation. Secondary legislation is also referred to as delegated legislation. The terms are synonymous. 'Delegated' refers to the fact that the powers claimed by the legislation are delegated from an item of primary legislation, i.e. its parent or enabling Act, which proscribes its powers and procedures.

Legislationc
has sub-classes

IRI: https://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/StatutoryInstrument

A statutory instrument. Statutory instruments are a type of delegated (or secondary) legislation. They can be used to make specific changes to the law under under authority contained in Acts of Parliament.

enablingActop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: https://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/enablingAct

Relates a piece of secondary legislation to its parent or enabling Act. Secondary legislation can have one or more enabling Acts. Secondary legislation can delegate powers to make further subordinate legislation. This is known as a sub-delegation or tertiary law. Such law is not usually made by statutory instrument, and doesn’t come near Parliament.

comingIntoForceDatedp back to ToC or Data Property ToC

IRI: https://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/comingIntoForceDate

The date on which the Statutory Instrument came, or is going to come, into force. Where given, this will be the date on which the whole Statutory Instrument comes into force. Sometimes coming into force will be fuzzier than a given date e.g. sometimes different schedules within the SI will come into force at different times. In these cases there'll be no comingIntoForceDate; the coming into force details will be given as a comingIntoForceNote.
